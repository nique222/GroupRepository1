Quiche

Like a pie but it's eggs

People have been doing weird things with eggs since the dawn of eggs. One of the best of those weird things is Quiche. Quiche is fluffy eggs in a flaky crust. You can add in whatever you want, but in this recipe we're going to add cheese, bacon, and caramelized onions.

Quiche Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 unbaked flaky pie crust (buy one from the store)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon each salt and pepper
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 lb of bacon
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Par-bake your crust at 375°F for 15 minutes.
  2. Cook the bacon in a pan over medium until just crispy. Chop roughly.
  3. Scoop out some (but not all) of the bacon fat with a spoon and throw the sliced onions into the pan. Stir occasionally over medium heat until the onions are deeply browned and cooked way down.
  4. In a bowl, beat together 4 eggs, whole milk, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  5. Whisk cheese, bacon, and onions into egg mixture.
  6. Pour the egg mixture into the pie crust.
  7. Cook at 350°F for 45-55 minutes or until the center is just about set.
  8. Allow to cool for 15 minutes and serve!
